For each labeled session, the prototype emits one compact CTI object. The object records the signal stream, evidence roles, implementation families, fired relations, epistemic status, confidence, actionability, recommended defensive use, and shareability boundary. Confidence is computed rather than assigned freely: the score combines the epistemic status, telemetry-profile reliability, evidence specificity, interaction depth, and relation support, and is capped by the maximum strength allowed by the epistemic status. Shareability is computed as a disclosure-risk score and materialized as two field sets: shareable evidence and non-shareable deception context. This makes the representation useful for recipient-side hunting and triage while withholding the exact deployment details that would reveal the defender's deception logic.
